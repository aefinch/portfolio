About

I grew up in Virginia, just outside of D.C., and attended Virginia Tech (Go Hokies!) to earn my Bachelor's in Mathematics. I wound up working in mortgages, of all things – the upside is I know all the ins and outs of buying a house, the downside is I have little passion for the work. I’ve always enjoyed logic puzzles, numbers games, and I had dabbled in cryptography and programming in the past out of curiosity. Programming clicked with me right away, the trouble was finding a way to learn it while working full time.

Call it luck or fate, but shortly after moving to Nashville I came across an article about Nashville Software School. I applied for the next available school session and was thrilled to be accepted. This is one of the best decisions I’ve ever made! I’m looking forward to a career that I’m incredibly passionate about and that encourages life-long learning.

I spend my free time either at home with my many pets or traveling, hiking, reading, and trying new foods. I hope to have more opportunities to travel internationally in the future; there’s a long list of places I want to go. I love trying new local restaurants and breweries (you could probably tell my foodie side inspired my featured projects). I’m passionate about the environment and would like to find ways to apply my developer skills to support environmental causes.

BoozeHound

BoozeHound is an online beer and wine journal. It uses the Google Maps API to find breweries or wineries based on a given location. Then the app accepts user input and pushes data up to Firebase.

Technologies Used: Bootstrap, AngularJS, Javascript, Google Maps API
